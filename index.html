Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between stamped concrete and concrete pavers?
There are many aspects that need to be looked at when making the comparison:

Maintenance: Stamped concrete requires very little maintenance. The only requirements are to reseal and clean on a routine basis. Concrete pavers on the other hand are more involved. They require reworking the joint sand periodically. On the other hand, concrete pavers need to be installed correctly. If they are not installed correctly settling of the pavers can occur. This will require extensive rework to fix and place the pavers back into place.

Design: Stamped concrete offers a wide variety of patterns and textures that resemble stone. Stamped concrete can also be installed with a custom color. Concrete pavers also have available a wide variety of colors, sizes, and shapes that resemble stone.

Cost: Concrete pavers and stammped concrete pricing is very similar. Both involve more complex and labor-intensive installation than concrete slabs. Therefore, they both are more expensive to install than a concrete patio. When considering long-term, concrete pavers typically cost less to repair. There, the long-term cost associated with concrete pavers can be lower than stamped concrete.

How long does it take for concrete to dry?
Concrete is always hardening. The concrete continually reacts with the water it is mixed with. It gradually gets harder and harder as the concrete particles react with water. The big question is how long does it take to cure to complete the activities for the purposes you installed the concrete for. Typically, concrete takes 24 to 48 hours to dry enough so you can walk of drive on it.

What is concrete resurfacing?
Concrete resurfacing is a thin layer of concrete that is applied over the top of concrete that contains binders that attach the new concrete layer to the existing concrete. The resurfacing agent is poured over the top of the concrete and creates a beautiful and durable smooth finish. The new surface gets stronger and stronger as it drys. Resurfacing works best on concrete that has minor damage on the surface. On Concrete the has major cracks and holes needs to have repair performed first on those areas before resurfacing.
